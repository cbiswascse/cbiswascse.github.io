About me

I like building things that work well — and figuring out why they break when they don't.

That curiosity started early: I grew up loving puzzles, and software turned out to be the same game with a bigger box. It's still what keeps me here — the quiet satisfaction of taking something tangled and making it click into place.

Today I'm a software engineer at CHI Deutschland Cargo Handling, building and maintaining web and mobile apps with Ruby on Rails and React. A lot of my focus lately has been on breaking a large monolith into a cleaner modular monolith using Domain-Driven Design — drawing clear bounded contexts, untangling tightly-coupled code, and leaning on Event Sourcing so the system is easier to reason about and change.

I work in a small, agile team, enjoy code reviews and pairing, and like sharing what I learn through internal talks. Outside the day-to-day, I'm happiest picking up something new that makes the product a little better for the people using it.
